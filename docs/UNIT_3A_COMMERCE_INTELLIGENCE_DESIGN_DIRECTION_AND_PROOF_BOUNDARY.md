- Inspiration is not implementation.
- Proof is not permission to invent claims.
- Public-safe data is not automatic website copy.
- Mockup metrics are not approved metrics.
- Design direction is not deployment.
- Preview deployment is not production cutover.

## 5. New design direction

The next concept should present DaDaStore as a premium commerce-growth agency combining performance marketing, creative strategy, and Shopify/CRO expertise. The design direction is:

- a dark luxury-tech interface
- a stronger, evidence-led proof and credibility layer
- sharper section hierarchy and more decisive editorial pacing
- advanced visual storytelling that explains growth systems rather than decorating the page
- a conversion-focused landing-page structure
- a clear connection between paid traffic, creative testing, measurement, and commerce journeys

“Commerce intelligence” is a positioning and storytelling direction. It must not imply proprietary AI, software, automation, or technology that does not exist.

## 6. Visual inspiration boundary

The supplied template/mockup may inspire:

- the split hero composition
- prominent but restrained proof/stat cards
- the dark blue, cyan, magenta, and violet gradient mood
- larger strategic positioning and clearer typography hierarchy
- grouped capability cards
- process or operating-system framing
- a high-contrast CTA block
- premium spacing and alternating dark/light section rhythm
- subtle motion and interaction ideas that remain accessible and dependency-free

The following mockup content must not be copied or treated as evidence:

- company metrics, including stores scaled, revenue generated, ROAS, satisfaction, or ad spend
- client logos or “trusted by” claims
- testimonial copy, identities, roles, ratings, or platform references
- case-study brands, products, charts, revenue lifts, ROAS, or CPA reductions
- awards, certifications, or partner claims
- exact third-party branding, product imagery, interface artwork, or page copy
- any unverifiable revenue-generated, ad-spend-managed, or performance result

The mockup is visual inspiration only. Its numerical claims and social proof are fictional or otherwise unapproved for DaDaStore publication.

## 8. Proof usage boundary

### Allowed public credibility

Subject to final Project Owner approval and accurate Upwork attribution:

- marketplace standing
- Upwork badge/status
- total earnings
- total jobs
- total hours
- profile rate, only if the Project Owner approves public display
- public profile title
- visible service and skill categories

### Allowed but requires careful wording

The following may be considered only after source-level review, privacy review, exact attribution, and Project Owner approval:

- short review excerpts
- completed-job categories
- portfolio categories or selected portfolio screenshots
- certifications and employment-history facts visible in the proof pack

Review excerpts must remain faithful to the source, must not expose private information, and must not be reframed as a broader performance guarantee. Portfolio evidence must not imply that every visible brand, asset, or result is owned by or publicly endorses DaDaStore.

### Not allowed without explicit Project Owner approval

- private client names
- private client messages
- screenshots containing sensitive client information
- revenue generated for clients
- ad spend managed
- ROAS, CPA, conversion-lift, or other performance metrics
- named case-study results
- client logos without documented public approval
- awards or certifications that are not both visible and approved
- any metric, testimonial, company, or result taken from the template/mockup

Final proof selection, exact labels, current-value revalidation, Upwork attribution, and publication all require Project Owner approval. The profile rate should remain omitted unless separately approved.

## 10. Recommended positioning

Recommended hero headline option:

> Commerce growth systems built for performance, creative testing, and Shopify scale.

Recommended supporting-copy option:

> DaDaStore helps ecommerce and service brands turn paid traffic, creative strategy, tracking, and conversion journeys into clearer growth systems.

This language is a planning option, not approved final copy. The Project Owner must decide whether the audience is ecommerce only or ecommerce plus service brands before implementation.

## 11. Recommended section structure

1. **Floating header** — approved identity, compact navigation, and one primary contact action.
2. **Hero with proof/stat cards** — new strategic positioning plus owner-approved marketplace facts, not mockup metrics.
3. **Credibility strip** — concise Upwork standing and verified service-category support; no client-logo strip without approval.
4. **Growth systems / services** — paid advertising, Shopify/CRO, creative strategy, and tracking/analytics grouped as an operating system.
5. **Commerce intelligence** — explain how signals from traffic, creative, tracking, offers, and customer journeys guide decisions; do not imply unbuilt software.
6. **Process / operating rhythm** — adapt the existing Audit, Strategy, Build, Launch, Optimize, Scale model into a clearer system narrative.
7. **Proof from Upwork** — use only owner-approved marketplace evidence with explicit attribution and careful privacy boundaries.
8. **Work frameworks / coming soon** — preserve placeholders or replace them with non-client framework cards only after the owner decision in Unit 3B.
9. **Contact CTA** — retain the approved mailto path unless a later decision unit changes the contact architecture.
10. **Footer** — approved brand presentation, service summary, and continuity links appropriate to a static site.

## 14. Technical boundary

The V3 direction must retain the current lightweight architecture unless a later planning unit explicitly changes it:

- static HTML
- plain CSS
- vanilla JavaScript
- no framework
- no build tooling
- no package manager
- no external animation library
- no analytics yet
- no contact backend yet
- no database, API submission, or persistence
- no production cutover

Any motion should use native CSS/JavaScript, respect reduced-motion preferences in both layers, and avoid worsening the recorded no-JavaScript and mobile-menu focus-management limitations.
